Who I am

Hi,

My name is Hoanh An and I am a software engineer. Before reading any further, let me tell you my story.

It all started when I was in high school. I was so obsessed with technology that I wanted to own every devices that I could reach. Everyday, I would go to different forums, buy and sell things online. I owned different types of Blackberry, Android phones and all kind of iPod and iPhone generation. I took a lot of time playing with them, understood them and then eventually started a business. Since it was the time that smartphones became to gain their popularity, the needs for used devices was also increased. I made money by buying and selling many of them, along with advising and troubleshooting services.

I did that for 3 years and stopped in 2016, right after I graduated high school, because I wanted to do something different.

I wanted to start a tech company, like Apple or Google. It was a very spontaneous moment. Sadly, I used to believe that I could hire engineers to work for me and all I needed to do was to lead them. I was wrong.

I was totally wrong. I found out what most founders had to start a successful company was a solid technical background. I did not have one. I did not know how to code nor know anything about computer architecture. I knew that I had to change.

I committed to work hard to become a great engineer. Time passed, I chose to devote my time and focus on software engineering since it attracted me more than anything else.

Right now, I am currently a sophomore at Bennington College where I study Math and Computer Science. I know, Bennington is not a technical school at all. It's not even in the top 10 liberal arts colleges. I will tell you why it fits me well.

Firstly, classes are small and professors are extremely supportive. Think about startups, they are small and performance can be measured so there’s a lot of potential for gain. Secondly, most professors are technical founders so they know what they are talking about when it comes to technical and non-technical problems. Last but not least, I would not let school interfere with my education. In other words, I am continuously learning new things everyday.

My lifetime mission is to either join a breakout startup or start my own. As long as I can constantly build stuff and work on challenging problems, I would be happy.

In my freetime, I enjoy building fun projects, honing my knowledge in science and engineering. Otherwise,

I would probably doing speedcubing, powerlifting, listening to lossless audio, or enjoying arthouse cinema. I talk about more about them in my blog.

Fact #1: When I was in secondary school, my personal record for 3x3 Rubik’s cube was around 15 seconds.

Fact #2: Right now, I am squatting and deadlifting 1.25 times my bodyweight and aiming to lift twice in the end of next year. I am hoping to build a solid strength then moving to Olympic weightlifting someday.

Fact #3: I would definitely invest in an expensive hi-fi audio system if I had “a small loan of a million dollars”

Fact #4: I think Eastern movies has more depth than Western’s since they are lack of popularity and sometimes underrated.

Virtual Pet

This project is to understand how micro-controller works. I’m using the Pro Trinket, instead of an Arduino. It’s big enough to fit the 3D print enclosure and powerful enough to run the project.

I call it “Virtual Pet” since it’s actually a pet. It responses to taps and vibration and shows 4 different emotions (neutral, sad, happy, angry) on its LED matrix face.

In normal circumstance, it gazes around blinks, plays spontaneous tones and patterns that correspond to its emotional state. It smiles when you play with its enough. If left alone for too long it gets lonely, but pester your pet too much and it may get angry.

If you have read my story, you would notice that I had 3-year experiences in Vietnamese online marketplace by buying and selling used phones. The product aimed to fill in the gap between people who want to exchange stuff, rather than selling and buying new ones. This would reduced the transactions costs since it required no 3rd party but still kept the product value high.

eStuff! is a startup for exchanging goods. An example will provide the best description of how it works.

Let’s say you have an iPhone 6 and want an iPhone 7. Other people have an iPhone 6 but want to downgrade for some reasons. Estuff! connects 2 of you to make the deal as easy as possible.
