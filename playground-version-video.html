Virtual Pet

This project is to understand how micro-controller works. I’m using the Pro Trinket, instead of an Arduino. It’s big enough to fit the 3D print enclosure and powerful enough to run the project.

I call it “Virtual Pet” since it’s actually a pet. It responses to taps and vibration and shows 4 different emotions (neutral, sad, happy, angry) on its LED matrix face.

In normal circumstance, it gazes around blinks, plays spontaneous tones and patterns that correspond to its emotional state. It smiles when you play with its enough. If left alone for too long it gets lonely, but pester your pet too much and it may get angry.

"eStuff! – was an incremental improvement of bartering and exchange web transactions about which he has already made significant progress. If you have read my story, you would notice that I had 3-year experiences in Vietnamese online marketplace by buying and selling used phones. The product aimed to fill in the gap between people who want to exchange stuff, rather than selling and buying new ones. This would reduced the transactions costs since it required no 3rd party but still kept the product value high.

eStuff! is a startup for exchanging goods. An example will provide the best description of how it works.

Let’s say you have an iPhone 6 and want an iPhone 7. Other people have an iPhone 6 but want to downgrade for some reasons. Estuff! connects 2 of you to make the deal as easy as possible.

Here is my launch plan that provides full details of my story (~3000 words): Naive eStuff's launch plan

GRE

This project was also accomplished at Techkids.vn. Along with PokemonQuiz, we made this GRE flashcards learning app because we wanted to study UITableView in Swift and build something more attractive than Quizlet.

The philosophy behind the app:

  1. It had to be simple, friendly colorful and easy to use.
  2. It had the learning process: Green line for Mastered Word (Right on first try), Yellow for Reviewing (Right on the second try), Red for Learning (Every wrong).
